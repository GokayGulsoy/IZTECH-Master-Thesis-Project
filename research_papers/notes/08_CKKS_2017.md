# CKKS (2017) — HE for Arithmetic of Approximate Numbers

**Citation**: Cheon, Kim, Kim, Song. *ASIACRYPT 2017*. Seoul National University + UCSD.

**One-line summary**: The original CKKS scheme — RLWE-based HE that natively supports approximate arithmetic over real/complex numbers via a novel **rescaling procedure**.

## Core contribution

- Treats encryption noise as part of the floating-point rounding error rather than something to eliminate.
- **Rescaling** truncates ciphertexts to smaller modulus → manages plaintext magnitude **and** rounds off LSBs simultaneously.
- Ciphertext modulus grows **linearly** with circuit depth (vs. exponentially in earlier exact-integer HE).
- Precision loss bounded by depth, only ~1 bit worse than unencrypted floating-point.
- Batching via complex canonical embedding (isometric ring homomorphism).

## Why CKKS for ML

- Native support for real-valued data (weights, activations).
- SIMD batching (N/2 slots per ciphertext) → amortized cost over many tokens.
- Demonstrated for inverse, exponential, logistic, DFT.

## Relevance to HyPER-LPAN

- **Foundation**. Every layer of HyPER-LPAN is evaluated in CKKS (RNS variant).
- Justifies token packing strategy: one ciphertext = one token, slot-batched over the 768-dim hidden representation.
- Cite as the foundational paper in §2 (background) and §3 (methodology).

## Direct citation use

- "We use the residue-number-system variant of CKKS [Cheon et al., ASIACRYPT'17; Cheon et al., 2018], which natively supports approximate arithmetic over real numbers and is the standard scheme for HE-based deep learning inference."
