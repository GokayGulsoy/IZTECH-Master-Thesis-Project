Literature Review Blueprint: FHE Polynomial
Approximations
Goal: Expand Section 2.3 (The Non-Linearity Bottleneck) and Section 3.3 (Polynomial
Approximations) of the Master's thesis.

Step 1: The Polynomial Baseline & CryptoNets
Before tackling modern Large Language Models, the thesis must establish the historical context
of replacing non-linear activation functions in neural networks.
 ●​ Target Paper: CryptoNets: Applying Neural Networks to Encrypted Data with High
    Throughput and Accuracy (Gilad-Bachrach et al., 2016).
 ●​ What to extract for Chapter 2:
    ○​ The justification for replacing the ReLU function.
    ○​ The introduction of the lowest-degree approximation: $f(x) = x^2$.
    ○​ The trade-off observed between this simple polynomial and the model's predictive
        accuracy.

Step 2: The Softmax and Attention Bottleneck
Moving from basic neural networks to Transformers, the complexity of the functions increases
drastically. The thesis must detail how researchers handle the division and exponentiation inside
the self-attention mechanism.
 ●​ Target Papers: * Iron: Homomorphic Evaluation of Transformer-based Models (Hao et al.,
    2023).
    ○​ THE-X: Privacy-Preserving Transformer Inference with Homomorphic Encryption (Chen
        et al., 2023).
 ●​ What to extract for Chapters 2 & 3:
    ○​ How the Softmax denominator (the sum of exponentials) is approximated to avoid FHE
        division.
    ○​ The use of Taylor Series expansions for $e^x$ and where they fail (Taylor series only
        approximate well near zero; attention scores often fall outside this radius).
    ○​ The impact of these approximations on natural language perplexity and BLEU scores.

Step 3: Advanced Mathematical Optimization (Minimax & Chebyshev)
This is the core theoretical contribution of your methodology. The thesis must prove that finding
the optimal polynomial is a rigorous mathematical process, not guesswork.
 ●​ Target Papers: * Look for recent papers from the Zama research team (e.g., Ilaria Chillotti)
    or search specifically for "Minimax approximation for homomorphic encryption machine
    learning."
 ●​ What to extract for Chapter 3:
    ○​ Chebyshev Interpolation: Why it solves the "Runge's phenomenon" (wild oscillations
       at the edges) that plagues Taylor series.
    ○​ The Remez Algorithm (Minimax): How this algorithm finds a polynomial $P(x)$ that
       minimizes the maximum absolute error compared to the true GELU/Softmax function
       over a specific interval $[-a, a]$.

Step 4: The Compiler Architecture
Since the implementation relies on concrete-ml executing on an RTX 5070 Ti, the thesis must
explain how these cryptographic circuits are actually compiled and optimized under the hood.
 ●​ Target Paper: CHET: An Optimizing Compiler for Fully-Homomorphic Neural-Network
    Inferencing (Dathathri et al., 2019).
 ●​ What to extract for Chapter 3:
     ○​ How FHE compilers automate tensor routing and parameter selection.
     ○​ The process of quantizing floating-point weights into integers before FHE circuit
         generation.
